# Clinical Guidelines: Diabetes Management

## Overview
Diabetes mellitus is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by elevated levels of blood glucose (blood sugar), which leads over time to serious damage to the heart, blood vessels, eyes, kidneys, and nerves.

## Key Management Guidelines
1. **Blood Sugar Monitoring**: Daily tracking of fasting and postprandial glucose levels is recommended.
2. **Dietary Recommendations**:
   - Focus on complex, high-fiber carbohydrates (e.g., whole grains, vegetables, legumes).
   - Avoid refined sugars, sweetened beverages, and high-glycemic foods.
   - Portion control is critical to managing glycemic load.
3. **Physical Activity**: At least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic physical activity per week (e.g., brisk walking, swimming, cycling).
4. **Key Medications**:
   - Metformin (often first-line therapy)
   - Insulin (for Type 1 diabetes and advanced Type 2)
   - Sulfonylureas (e.g., Glipizide)

## Warning Signs & Emergencies
- **Hypoglycemia (Low Blood Sugar)**: Shakiness, sweating, confusion, dizziness, rapid heart rate. Action: Take 15g of fast-acting glucose (e.g., fruit juice, candy) immediately.
- **Hyperglycemia (High Blood Sugar)**: Extreme thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, blurred vision. If accompanied by nausea or confusion, seek immediate medical attention (potential DKA).
