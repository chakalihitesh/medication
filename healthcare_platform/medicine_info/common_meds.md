# Medication Directory: Common Medicines

## 1. Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)
- **Uses**: Pain relief (analgesic) and fever reduction (antipyretic).
- **Dosage**: Standard adult dose is 500mg to 1000mg every 4 to 6 hours. Maximum daily dose is 4000mg (4g) in a 24-hour period.
- **Precautions**: Avoid alcohol as it increases risk of liver damage. Do not take with other products containing acetaminophen.

## 2. Metformin
- **Uses**: Controls blood sugar levels in Type 2 Diabetes patients.
- **Dosage**: Often started at 500mg twice daily, taken with meals to reduce gastrointestinal side effects.
- **Precautions**: Risk of lactic acidosis (rare but serious). Avoid excessive alcohol. Monitor kidney function regularly.

## 3. Lisinopril
- **Uses**: Treats high blood pressure (hypertension) and heart failure.
- **Dosage**: Usually starts at 10mg once daily.
- **Precautions**: Can cause a dry, persistent cough. Monitor blood potassium levels. Avoid in pregnancy due to risk of fetal harm.

## 4. Ibuprofen
- **Uses**: Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) for mild-to-moderate pain, inflammation, and fever.
- **Dosage**: Standard adult dose is 200mg to 400mg every 4 to 6 hours, up to 1200mg/day over-the-counter or 3200mg/day prescription.
- **Precautions**: Take with food to avoid stomach upset. Long-term use can increase risks of stomach ulcers, kidney problems, or cardiovascular events.
