# Clinical Guidelines: Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

## Overview
Hypertension is defined as persistent blood pressure readings of 130/80 mmHg or higher. It is a major risk factor for cardiovascular disease, stroke, and kidney failure.

## Key Management Guidelines
1. **DASH Diet (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension)**:
   - High in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.
   - Low in sodium (salt limit of less than 2,300 mg per day, ideally 1,500 mg).
   - Rich in potassium, calcium, and magnesium.
2. **Weight Management**: Maintain a healthy BMI. Every 1 kg weight loss reduces blood pressure by approximately 1 mmHg.
3. **Alcohol & Smoking**: Limit alcohol consumption and cease smoking completely.
4. **Common Medications**:
   - Lisinopril (ACE inhibitor)
   - Amlodipine (Calcium channel blocker)
   - Losartan (ARB)
   - Metoprolol (Beta blocker)

## Warning Signs & Emergencies
- **Hypertensive Crisis**: Blood pressure exceeding 180/120 mmHg.
- **Symptoms**: Severe headache, chest pain, shortness of breath, back pain, numbness/weakness, difficulty speaking, or vision changes.
- **Action**: Seek emergency medical care immediately.
